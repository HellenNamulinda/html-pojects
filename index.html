Nelson Mandela

An icon of democracy and social justice

Here is a time line of Mandela's life:

  • 1918 - Born in Mvezo, Umtata, South Africa
  • 1933 - Began his secondary education at Clarkebury Methodist High School in Engcobo which he completed in two years.
  • 1937 - Moved to Healdtown the Methodist college in Fort Beaufort, where he became increasingly interested in native African culture.
  • 1939 - Began to work on a BA degree at the University of Fort Hare, an elite black institution in Alice, Eastern Cape
  • 1941 - Worked as a night watchman at Crown Mines.
  • 1943 - Returned to Johannesburg after passing his BA exams to follow a political path as a lawyer. Began studying law at the University of the Witwatersrand
  • 1944 - Joins the African National Congress and founds the Youth League. Marries Evelyn Mase.
  • 1950 - took Xuma's place on the ANC national executive and that same year was elected national president of the ANCYL
  • 1952 - Establishes South Africaâ€™s first black law firm with Oliver Tambo, Mandela & Tambo.
  • 1958 - Divorces Evelyn Mase and marries Winnie Madikizela.
  • 1961 - Founds in association with the South African Communist Party the militant Umkhonto we Sizwe and leads a sabotage campaign against the apartheid.
  • 1962 - Is arrested and imprisoned. Subsequently sentenced to life imprisonment for conspiring to overthrow the state.
  • 1985 - Refuses to be released , after president P. W. Botha, offers to free him, saying the government must first dismantle apartheid.
  • 1990 - Is released from prision and gives a speech declaring his commitment to peace and reconciliation with the white minority.
  • 1993 - Wins the Nobel Peace Prize.
  • 1994 - Becomes President of South Africa after negotiating an end to apartheid.
  • 1995 - Encourages black South Africans to get behind the previously hated national rugby team, the Springboks, as South Africa hosts the Rugby World Cup.
  • 2013 - Dies at the age of 95 on December 5th, after suffering from a prolonged respiratory infection.
